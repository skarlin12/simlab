What This Demo Shows

Fixed programming relies on pre-defined pick points. When objects are tilted, overlapping, or in unexpected positions, the arm either misses the grasp or triggers an exception requiring human intervention. This causes 10-20% of picks to fail, with each failure costing thousands in downtime.

GI's inverse dynamics predicts object pose and adapts grasp strategy in real-time. The world model reasons about contact physics and gripper dynamics, achieving successful picks even in the "unstructured 20%" that causes most production downtime.
